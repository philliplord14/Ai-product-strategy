### Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):**  
K.i Pro turns Kallidus's 25 years of proprietary performance and outcomes 
data into a proactive intelligence layer that tells L&D teams, managers and 
employees what to do next — before problems occur — shifting L&D from a 
cost centre to a measurable driver of business performance.

**The case:**

1. **Why now:** AI is becoming an expectation across HR tech, not a 
   differentiator — generic tools are commoditising fast. The window to 
   establish a defensible, data-grounded proactive intelligence product 
   is open now but closing. Customers are already building shadow AI 
   workarounds (ChatGPT for development plans, Copilot for wellbeing 
   signals) — K.i Pro needs to absorb these behaviours before they 
   normalise. Skills strategy and AI strategy appear in ~90% of RFPs, 
   and 180 customers adopted K.i skills capability in 12 months — 
   demand is proven.

2. **What's defensible:** The 25-year proprietary non-PII dataset 
   spanning performance outcomes, learning, skills and compliance is 
   not replicable by any competitor or new entrant. Deep platform 
   integration across performance, learning and compliance means 
   K.i Pro can make cross-domain inferences no point solution can 
   match. The outcomes-first framing — strategy builder returns a 
   prioritised plan, not a content catalogue — has no direct market 
   competition today.

3. **The economics:** £1M investment secured. Dedicated squad in place. 
   First chargeable feature targeted for Q2 FY27 (6 months post-chatbot 
   beta). Advisory board generates early revenue commitment before 
   general availability. Existing warm customer base = low acquisition 
   cost for upsell. Payback period modelled at 1–2 years if pricing 
   and attach rate targets are met.

**The risks:**

1. **Trust / failure modes:** K.i Pro operates in a high-stakes 
   employment context — an inaccurate burnout signal or fabricated 
   compliance alert damages trust immediately. Mitigation: golden 
   dataset, tiered confidence UX, human-in-loop architecture and 
   HITL governance are all defined. EU AI Act compliance review 
   is a hard gate before commercial launch.

2. **Scale / governance:** Azure OpenAI token costs at production 
   scale are unmodelled. Unit economics must be confirmed before 
   pricing is fixed. Green light / red light governance model is 
   defined to prevent every feature requiring full board review 
   — but governance board sign-off remains a hard gate for 
   high-risk features.

3. **Competitive:** Thrive's Kiki is fully agentic now and included 
   at no extra cost — the pricing asymmetry is the most immediate 
   threat. Zensai's Microsoft 365 integration is a structural 
   platform risk. MCP standardisation could commoditise the 
   intelligence layer if K.i Pro's answer is not the proprietary 
   data depth, not just the presence of AI.

**The ask:**  
Sign-off on advisory board launch date, pricing model and legal review 
completion as hard gates for Q1 FY27. Board confirmation that the 
dedicated squad remains ring-fenced. Alignment on the broad commercial 
launch window post-Phase 4 (2H FY27).

### M1 Baseline vs. Now

**M1 baseline:**  
K.i Pro is a proactive AI intelligence layer built on Kallidus's 
25-year data asset, designed to surface the right nudge and insight 
for learners, managers and L&D teams before problems occur — shifting 
L&D from activity reporting to measurable business outcomes.

**Now:**  
The strategic direction is right and the data moat is real — but the 
course has sharpened where the execution risk actually lives. The 
feedback loops are broken and need fixing before more features ship. 
The flywheel only compounds if each output makes the next one smarter 
— right now it doesn't. The shadow AI audit shows customers are already 
solving the problem with generic tools, which means the timer is running. 
The governance and trust architecture is now designed properly rather 
than assumed. And the competitive threat is more specific and more urgent 
than the original strategy acknowledged — Thrive is not a future risk, 
it is a live one.
