## Scores

### Contextual Moat — 4/5
*Workflow depth × switching cost. Would users leave in a weekend if a competitor showed up?*

**Score rationale:** Having learning and performance data connected is a key market diffrienciator and although learning and reviews data can be exported the links are lost in the connected data to be able to prove ROI of how learning and skills development impacted performance and global outcomes

**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Thrive, Cornerstone, Learning pool. As they are storming ahead with the message of the same strategy, unable to do the same as only a Learning product but we are winning new business from these competitors frequently 
---

### Data Advantage — 4/5
*Proprietary signal that compounds with usage. What do you see that OpenAI doesn't?*

**Score rationale:** 25 years of priopriatry data however some data isn't as mature such as skills data were this is a new feature set in the last 18 months where customers are still adopting to get the rich data - however the strategy is a key driver to drive adoption too

**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Sana labs, Learning pool, 360 Learning
All have skills capabilty and data but not the length of historical data
---

### Platform Exposure — 3/5
*Encroachment risk × pivot speed. If Apple/Google/OpenAI ships your hero feature native — then what?*

**Score rationale:** The largest risk currently is chatbots and MCP agents where the data can be pulled to and as an industry there is talk over moving to chat/ agent first learning. However this is very unknown currently and at the core there is still and laways will be a need for manditory training and compliance. Building out a full LMS capabilty would take many years, skills products are already available and a competative market. The true value that cant be replicated is the history and connectivity of data. The main risk though remains whether the market moves to a headless/ chat version for end users

**Named attacker (from partner challenge):**
Thrive
